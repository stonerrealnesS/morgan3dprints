3D printing is an additive manufacturing process that creates objects layer by layer from a digital 3D model. It allows highly customized, complex items to be made quickly and affordably.

A digital 3D model is sliced into thin layers by software. The printer melts plastic filament and deposits it layer by layer until the final object is complete.

Almost anything: prototypes and replacement parts, toys, tools, art, custom gifts, educational models, business products, and more. 3D printing is used in engineering, manufacturing, medical, architecture, hobbies, and schools.

Common materials include PLA, PETG, ABS/ASA, TPU (flexible), and specialty filaments like glow-in-the-dark, metallic, silk, or wood-filled. Each material has different strengths and ideal uses.

Strength depends on the material and how the item is designed. Many prints are durable enough for everyday use, displays, hardware, brackets, and functional parts. We can help choose the right material and orientation for your use case.

Prints can take anywhere from minutes to multiple hours depending on size, detail, and complexity. Small items might finish in under 20 minutes, while large pieces may take many hours or even overnight.

Not typically. Costs are mainly based on material, time, and design complexity. For custom or low-volume items, 3D printing is often cheaper and faster than traditional manufacturing methods or tooling.

PLA (the most common filament) is plant-based and biodegradable under industrial composting conditions. We also minimize waste where possible, and many prints can be reused or recycled depending on your local facilities.

PLA can soften in high heat, such as a hot car or direct summer sun. PETG, ASA, and ABS are better choices for outdoor use, higher temperatures, or harsher environments. We can recommend the right material for your project.

Modern printers can be very accurate — we can produce details as small as about 0.08 mm (thinner than a sheet of paper), depending on the geometry and material.
